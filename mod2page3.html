  • Skeletal alignment is a fancy way of saying posture.
  • Muscles that are attached on either side of a joint work together.
  • Muscle imbalance is when the shorter or stronger muscle starts to pull that body out of position and your whole body must compensate.
  • Your muscles are what move your skeleton so it makes sense that adjustments in your muscles lead to adjustments in skeletal alignment.
  • It is important to meet with a licensed chiropractor who will work with you on structural alignment.
  • Just as important is to work with a physical therapist who will work on your muscle symmetry in order to help your muscles work to keep those skeletal adjustments in place.
