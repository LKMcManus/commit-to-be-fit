Alright you are ready to start losing weight, but is that what you really want? You could actually lose a lot of your muscle mass and weigh less but have more fat. What we typically mean when we say we want to lose weight is we want less fat on our bodies. Men need a minimum of 3% fat to remain healthy and women need 12%. Trained professionals can administers caliper tests, bioelectrical impedance analysis or BOD POD measurements that would isolate the fat composition of your body but seriously who has time/money for that? The keep it simple method is to monitor your BMI and waist circumference. Body Mass Index just means your weight relative to your height. Check out the link and chart to assess your starting point. A BMI of 25 or Higher is overweight, 30 and higher is obese.
